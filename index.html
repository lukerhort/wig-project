About the Project

We are a team of four aerospace engineering students at SDSU building a radio-controlled Wing in Ground Effect (WIG) vehicle. A WIG vehicle flies very close to the surface, where ground proximity disrupts wingtip vortices, reduces induced drag, and increases lift relative to open-air flight. Our prototype demonstrates this effect at RC scale using a 3D-printed airframe over carbon fiber spars, four outboard propeller motors, and a Holybro Pixhawk 6C flight controller running ArduPlane.

Powered by four BrotherHobby Tornado T5 3115 Pro motors with Skywalker V2 ESCs (Electronic Speed Controllers) and Ovonic 6S 5200mAh LiPo batteries, the vehicle produces 28 lb of static maximum thrust at a total weight of 20 lb, for a thrust-to-weight ratio of 1.4. Target flight time is 20 minutes.

Design Specifications

Airfoil: FX 63-137 — high-lift, low-speed profile, well-suited for ground effect vehicle designs

Wingspan: 5.4 ft (5.8 ft with winglets)  |  Aspect Ratio: 5.4

Mean Chord: 12 in  |  Wing Area: 5.4 ft²  |  Outboard Anhedral: 15°

Motors: 4× BrotherHobby Tornado T5 3115 Pro (outboard, tractor configuration)

Propellers: 4× 3-blade 8×4.5 in  |  ESCs: 4× Skywalker V2 Series

Batteries: 2× Ovonic 6S 5200mAh LiPo  |  PDB: Matek X Class 12S (Power Distribution Board)

Flight Controller: Holybro Pixhawk 6C running ArduPlane, dual GPS

Rangefinder: Benewake TFmini-S LiDAR altimeter for ride height measurement

RC System: RadioMaster Boxer + ELRS receiver  |  Telemetry: 3DR 500mW 915MHz radio kit

Structure: 3D-printed ASA/ABS over carbon fiber spar skeleton

Servos: 5× Hosyond DS3225 25kg high-torque metal gear  |  UBEC: 5V/6V regulated BEC for avionics power

Bench Testing

We tested the motors and propellers in SDSU's low-speed wind tunnel to measure maximum static thrust. A custom aluminum mount was machined and fitted with a load cell to record thrust output at varying throttle levels across all four motor and propeller combinations. Bench testing validates performance before flight and helps catch mechanical or electrical problems early, before the airframe is at risk.

We also completed all power system soldering: the four Skywalker V2 ESCs were soldered to the Matek X Class 12S PDB (Power Distribution Board), along with the power module and the UBEC (Universal Battery Eliminator Circuit) supplying regulated 5V power to the avionics stack. Measured static max thrust across all four motors: 28 lb.

Project Updates

April 13, 2026

Taxi Test

The fully assembled WIG vehicle completed its first taxi test. With all four motors running, the aircraft tracked straight on the ground and responded correctly to control inputs from the RadioMaster Boxer. Control surfaces and motor arming all checked out nominal. The airframe held up without issue under motor thrust loading.

April 9, 2026

Full Assembly and Full System Test

Full airframe assembly completed. All electronics, wiring, servos, and avionics installed and integrated. Completed a full system test including motor arming, ESC (Electronic Speed Controller) calibration, control surface travel checks, and Pixhawk sensor validation. Flight controller configured in ArduPlane with telemetry link established.

Late March 2026

Motor Testing & Final Build Push

Motor and propeller bench testing completed at SDSU's low-speed wind tunnel. All wing sections, fuselage, tail, and motor housing nacelles finished printing. Full assembly and wiring underway.

Early March 2026

Weeks 7–9: Full Build Phase Underway

To cut down on print times and eliminate dependence on shared lab printers, the team purchased a Bambu Lab P2S — dramatically reducing turnaround per part. Fuselage sections 1–4 and section 6 are complete (40 hrs done); nose cone and section 5 are still printing, with wings and tail queued after. Wiring diagram finalized. Control surfaces designed and nose cone redesigned; servo hatches in progress.

Bench testing motors underway — measuring thrust output, amp draw, motor and ESC temperature, and checking for voltage sag under load. Full system integration bench test planned once remaining parts arrive. Still on order: Pixhawk 6C, 3DR Radio Telemetry Kit, RadioMaster ELRS Receiver.

February 23, 2026

Weeks 5–6: Parts Arrived, Printing Started

Motors, ESCs, batteries, propellers, carbon fiber spars, and all filament arrived. Nose cone, fuselage sections 1–3, and wing section 1 completed on the printer.

February 6, 2026

Week 4: Design Finalized, Proposal Submitted

Design locked: FX 63-137 airfoil, AR=5.4, 5.4ft wingspan, 4-motor distributed propulsion. Full SolidWorks assembly completed. Proposal submitted and approved.

January 2026

Weeks 1–3: Research & Trade Studies

Researched historical WIG vehicles (Caspian Sea Monster, Lun class, Orlyonok). Completed airfoil trade study comparing FX 63-137, NACA 4412, and GAW-1. Selected FX 63-137 for its superior low-speed high-lift characteristics.
